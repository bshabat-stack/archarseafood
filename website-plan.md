# Archar Seafood — Website Plan
*July 20, 2026 · Inspiration source: madisonseafood.net (structure only, not positioning)*

## Positioning

Madison Seafood is a wholesale distributor; its site is a storefront funnel ("Shop Now" → Freshline). Archar is a 41-year-old neighborhood market whose transaction happens by phone and in person. Our site is a **trust and traffic engine, not a checkout**: it exists to (1) get retail customers to call or visit, (2) capture wholesale inquiries, and (3) own local search for "seafood market near me / Somerset NJ."

**Brand pillars → proof points:**

| Pillar | Proof we have | Proof we need from Michael |
|---|---|---|
| Freshness | "Fresh fish daily" signage, filleted in front of you, reviews praising multi-day freshness | Daily/weekly delivery cadence, sourcing ports |
| Hometown feel | 41 years on Hamilton St, Michael's reputation in reviews, 4.9★ | Founding story, family involvement, owner photo |
| Sustainability | Wild-caught (community Q&A) | Actual sourcing practices, any certifications — do NOT overclaim |

## What we take from Madison — and what we do differently

**Take:** clean 3-step process narrative; popular-items grid; dedicated sustainability page; photo strip; simple footer with hours/contact.

**Do differently (their gaps = our edge):**

1. Their sustainability page is one quote + 3 external links. Ours gets real content (wild-caught sourcing, seasonal honesty) once Michael confirms specifics.
2. They have zero personality — no story, no faces. Michael IS the brand; reviews prove it. We lead with him.
3. No reviews/social proof anywhere on their site. We have a 4.9★ average and quotable superlatives ("worth the 1.5-hour drive").
4. Their CTA requires an account and delivery zone. Ours is one tap: call (732) 249-5835.
5. Local SEO: they target wholesale buyers; nobody in this area owns "fresh seafood market Somerset NJ" organically. We will.

## Sitemap (launch = one page with anchors; later = split pages)

1. **Hero** — logo, "Fresh fish daily since 1983" (pending confirmation), Somerset's neighborhood fish market. CTA: Call / See Today's Catch.
2. **The Archar Way (3-step)** — Sourced wild-caught daily → Cut to order in front of you → Steamed & seasoned on request (their steaming service is a differentiator Madison can't match).
3. **Fresh Catch grid** — species they actually stock: blue claw crabs, porgies, whiting, snapper, croakers, tilapia, catfish, salmon, sea bass, shrimp, clams, oysters, lobster. "By the bushel / by the case" retail-wholesale callout.
4. **Blue Crab feature** — signature item; live crabs by the bushel, seasonal messaging.
5. **Our Story** — 41 years, Michael, hometown Somerset/East Franklin.
6. **Sustainability** — wild-caught commitment; placeholder pending owner input.
7. **Wholesale** — restaurants & hotels supplied, delivery, custom cutting; inquiry form.
8. **Reviews strip** — 3 real quotes + 4.9★ badges.
9. **Visit / Contact** — hours (flag Fri–Sat 6pm question), address, click-to-call, map link, inquiry form.

## Functional decisions (agreed)

- Primary CTA: **click-to-call**, with a lightweight inquiry form (retail pre-orders + wholesale) — no e-commerce at launch.
- Audience: retail-first homepage, distinct wholesale section.
- Deliverable now: this plan + single-page HTML prototype for Michael's review.

## Content gaps blocking final copy (owner interview list)

1. Confirm founding year (~1983) and the story behind the name "Archar."
2. Owner's full name; permission to feature him; staff consent for counter photos.
3. Sustainability specifics — sourcing, wild vs farmed by species. No claims without this.
4. Fri–Sat closing time (5 or 6pm). An email address for the inquiry form.
5. Original photos: daytime storefront, Michael behind the counter, crab bushels. Current images are review-sourced placeholders — replace before launch.
6. Domain purchase (check archarseafood.com) and Google Business Profile ownership.

## Phased roadmap

- **Phase 1 (now):** prototype → Michael review → photo shoot + interview → swap placeholders.
- **Phase 2 (launch):** domain, hosting, form backend, Google Business Profile link-up, basic local schema (LocalBusiness JSON-LD).
- **Phase 3 (later, only if Michael wants it):** today's-catch price board he can update by phone/text, online pre-orders, wholesale portal.
